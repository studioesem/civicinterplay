# Civic Interplay: Design Context

## Users

Practitioners in civic tech, design, and cultural policy who are curious about AI but not yet sure what to do with it. Students and academics in adjacent fields. People who think reflectively about technology and don't want a tech-bro framing or a doom framing.

They arrive via newsletter, social link, or word of mouth, expecting to read. They should leave with something they can *use*: a tool, a worksheet, a prompt structure, a way of thinking about a skill or a markdown file, and eventually a workshop they can join or run.

The site is a **Think + Do Space**. The *Do* matters as much as the *Think*. Tools live on subdomains (e.g. `sightings.civicinterplay.io`); the main site is the gallery, the lobby, and the editorial.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **playful-rigorous, generative, relational.**

- Not serious, a bit serious. The work asks real questions about planetary citizenship and AI sovereignty, but invites readers in with humour, hand-made energy, and curiosity rather than gravitas.
- Voice is essayistic, contemplative, occasionally mystical, never preachy. Italics for emphasis, not bold-shouting.
- Everything is framed as a draft, an experiment, an invitation. No finality language.
- The site should feel like a research notebook opening up to others, not a personal blog and not a finished product.

## Aesthetic Direction

Editorial / zine / risograph register. Bold 2px borders, offset solid box-shadows (`4px 4px 0`), card-as-panel layout, hand-set typography energy without being twee.

- **Theme:** light mode (warm grey `#f8f8f8`). Content is reflective long-form reading; people sit with these essays.
- **Type:** Fira Sans (headings), Merriweather (body). Already chosen, no rework needed.
- **Palette:** purple `#7D50BD`, pink `#E076DB`, terracotta `#D16D54`, blush `#F8DFF6`, sand `#DACCBA`, forest `#454E41`, periwinkle `#8E9BDD`.
- **Category colours:** Introduction = purple, Training Grounds = terracotta, The Guides = periwinkle, Work Sheets = forest.
- **Signature moves:** glitch hover on key words; scroll-revealed callouts with coloured left borders rotating through the palette; offset solid shadows on bordered panels; gradient-animated header.
- **Anti-references:** generic Substack template, slick agency portfolio, academic department CMS, glossy AI-startup landing page, dashboard / admin UI in tasteful greys.
- **Accessibility:** WCAG AA target, full keyboard nav, focus rings on all interactive surfaces, `prefers-reduced-motion` honoured (kill scroll-reveal and glitch animations when set).

## Design Principles

1. **Take-away over read-through.** Every page should make it easy to leave with a tool, worksheet, prompt, or next step. The home and section pages are gallery + lobby, not just an archive of posts. Tools (e.g. `sightings.civicinterplay.io`) get first-class entry points, not a "links" footer.

2. **Resource for others, not a personal site.** The architecture should accommodate contributions, workshops, and tools that aren't authored by Sarah without restructuring later. Avoid hard-coding "by Sarah" framing into nav, footers, or templates. The byline is a field, not a constant.

3. **Playful-rigorous, never preachy.** Visual moves (glitch, offset shadows, colour rotation) carry humour and craft. Copy invites enquiry. Neither the design nor the language should signal *finished* or *expert*.

4. **Honour the approved preview.** `civicinterplay-preview.html` is the source of truth for typography, palette, callouts, and card patterns. The migration is a port, not a redesign. Net-new components (tools index, workshops, contributor pages) should extend this language, not invent a new one.

5. **Light, varied rhythm.** Different sections use different grids (featured-asymmetric, two-up guides, auto-fill standard) so the page is not a uniform card carpet. Spacing breathes and varies; nothing centred for centring's sake.

## Skill Overrides

The `/impeccable` skill bans `border-left` greater than 1px on cards, callouts, and alerts. This site **overrides that ban deliberately on the `.callout` element only**:

- The 8px coloured left border is colour-coded (rotates through purple / periwinkle / terracotta / pink / forest), paired with a solid offset box-shadow, and reads as zine / manifesto, not dashboard alert.
- Approved by the site owner; encoded as a load-bearing visual feature.
- Future skill passes (`animate`, `polish`, `audit`, `quieter`, etc.) must not "fix" this.

All other ban patterns (gradient text, coloured side-stripes on regular cards, monoculture font reflexes) remain in force.
